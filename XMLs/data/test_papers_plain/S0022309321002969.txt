                  A new series of Er3+ ions doped Oxy Chloro Boro Tellurite glasses were prepared by melt-quenching method. Various spectroscopic properties were determined from absorption, excitation and photo-luminescence spectra. The Judd-Ofelt intensity parameters Ωλ(λ=2,4,6) were estimated based on absorption spectra. A strong luminescence band was observed at 1536nm under the excitation wavelength of 522nm. The gain coefficient of 1.53μm emission was estimated to probe the utility of glasses in broad band amplification technology. Inokuti-Hirayama model was employed to decay curves, to comprehend the nature of energy transfer between erbium ions. The stimulated emission cross-sections (σsec), branching ratios (βR), quantum efficiencies (η%) and gain cross-section (σsecxτR) for Er3+ ions doped OCBT glasses were evaluated and compared with other reported glass samples. Based on the highest stimulated emission cross-section, branching ratios, quantum efficiencies and gain cross-sections, it is suggested that 1mol% of Er3+ ions doped OCBT glass is more suitable for NIR region optical device applications.

A new series of Er3+  ions doped Oxy Chloro Boro Tellurite glasses were prepared by melt-quenching method. Various spectroscopic properties were determined from absorption, excitation and photo-luminescence spectra. The Judd-Ofelt intensity parameters Ωλ (λ=2,4,6) were estimated based on absorption spectra. A strong luminescence band was observed at 1536nm under the excitation wavelength of 522nm. The gain coefficient of 1.53μm emission was estimated to probe the utility of glasses in broad band amplification technology. Inokuti-Hirayama model was employed to decay curves, to comprehend the nature of energy transfer between erbium ions. The stimulated emission cross-sections (σsec ), branching ratios (βR ), quantum efficiencies (η%) and gain cross-section (σsec xτR ) for Er3+  ions doped OCBT glasses were evaluated and compared with other reported glass samples. Based on the highest stimulated emission cross-section, branching ratios, quantum efficiencies and gain cross-sections, it is suggested that 1mol% of Er3+  ions doped OCBT glass is more suitable for NIR region optical device applications.   Keywords Er3+  ions  Borotellurite glasses  J-O parameters  1.53 μm NIR emission    1 Introduction In recent years, researchers are showing great interest towards the optical materials, in order to address the technological challenges in the platform of photoluminescence device utility in solid state lasers, opto electronic devices, optical unit displays, optical amplifiers, up-conversion and down conversion amplifiers and many more [1] . It is well notable that the concentration, dissemination of rare earth ions and local environment of parent glass matrix holds the positive effect in flourishing the luminescence features like emission cross section, intensity, average life span and spectral line width. Hence, these characteristics are considered as the salient features in choice of rare earth ions doped glasses for luminescence device applications [2] . In terms of oxide glasses, borate glasses are considered as noted materials by their stupendous physical and optical properties such as optical transparency, low melting point, good rare earth ion solubility, high chemical and thermal stability and different coordination number [3] . Boron trioxide is a fine glass former that owns the ability to exist in both 3 & 4-coordinated environment with strong covalent B-O bond and is capable to form stable glasses. The only demerit is stretching vibrations produced in oxide glasses results in the exhibition of elevated phonon energies (~1400cm−1 ) and low refractive index [4] , which ultimately leads to non radiative process via multiphonon relaxation mechanisms, which further shows negative impact in rare earth (RE3+ ) ion photo luminescence and curbs the wide utility of borate glasses in photonic device applications. This demerit can be retrieved by treating the borate glasses with heavy metal oxides with low phonon energies, such as TeO2  (<800cm−1 ). TeO2  shows transmission region between 0.3-1.6μm and the glass gets stability by the activation of TeO2  into the host glass matrix [5] . The dopant Al2 O3  brings high strength, hardness and outstanding thermal resistance to the host matrix [6] . The geometric allocation of RE elements can be accomplished by the addition of Al2 O3  in order to prevent the clustering among them, which in turn improves the quality of the optical devices and finds wide practical application like optical lenses and windows, optical waveguides, antireflection coatings and many more [7] . The dopant BaCl2  makes the host glass transparent in IR region [8] . Addition of SrO brings structural stability in the host matrix [9] . The addition of modifiers to the host glass brings structural changes, such as transition of BO3  units into BO4  units and the structure becomes more open due to the non-bridging oxygen atoms enhancement. Due to its good rare earth ion solubility and flexible structure, borate glasses are emerging as potential materials for fabrication of new luminescent device [10]  such as lasers, solar up-converters, optical amplifiers, thermo luminescence dosimeters and emission devices [11] . All these applications of borate glasses are possible because of their random structure and excellent optical properties. The radiative quantum efficiency and lifetime are the two vital parameters of the host glass for exhibition of numerous applications. But for attaining good optical properties, the intensity of emission color and proper doping of RE ions in glasses becomes important aspect [12] .   Quite recently, up-convert materials are gaining much attention because it owes capability of converting infrared light to visible light and perceives noticeable applications like color displays, optoelectronic devices, fiber amplifiers, optical data storage and under water optical communications [13] .    Now-a-days wavelength division multiplexing (WDM) technology is showing tremendous utility in telecommunications because, it transfers large amount of optical data for longer distance. So, broadband fiber amplifiers are the prominent devices in manufacturing the luminescence exhibiting materials between 1200nm to 2600nm. To consummate broad band utility, the host glass should be activated by RE ions. Among various lanthanide ions, Er3+  is one of the significant candidates with metastable energy levels with long lifetime and large absorption cross section at 980 nm. It evinces light in visible and NIR region at 1.5μm by exciting the band at 980nm [14] . The most important characteristic feature of Er3+  ions in infrared (NIR) emission at 1.5 μm through the 4 I13/2 →4 I15/2  transition and finds numerous applications in telecommunication such as frequency up-conversion lasers, waveguide lasers, waveguide amplifiers, eye safe laser systems, full color solid state displays, wave length division multiplexing (WDM), network system, erbium-doped fiber amplifier (EDFA) and lidar transmitters [15] . The current work is mainly focused on the physical and spectroscopic properties of erbium ion doped oxy chloro boro tellurite glasses, which unquestionably exist their application in the platform of technologies like solid state lasers and erbium doped fiber amplifiers etc.  2 Materials and Synthesis method In the current work, different concentrations of Er3+ doped oxy chloro boro tellurite glasses were prepared using chemicals such as strontium carbonate (SrCO3 ), aluminum trioxide (Al2 O3 ), boron trioxide (B2 O3 ), barium choride (BaCl2 ), tellurium dioxide (TeO2 ) and erbium trioxide (Er2 O3 ). Moreover, the emission line strengths related to the transitions from the upper multiplet manifolds 2S+1 LJ  relative to the lower multiplet manifolds of the erbium (Er3+ ) ions of the host glass matrix can be evaluated by using the JO intensity parameters. The emission transition 4 I13/2 →4 I15/2  of Er3+ ion possesses both magnetic and electric dipole moments. The magnetic dipole parameter doesn't depend on the ligand field, but it is responsible to the origin of intensified peak 4 I13/2 →4 I15/2 . The magnetic dipole(Smd ) line strength is generally considered as the characteristic of transition, which can be evaluated by using quantum numbers and can affect the emission properties of electric dipole transition(Sed ). By using J-O parameters, the line strength of electric dipole transition can be measured by the expression (Sed ) (4 I13/2 →4 I15/2 ) = 0.0195Ω2 +0.1172Ω4 +1.4325Ω6  and is dependent on structure of host glass matrix [46] .  3.4 Analysis of optical band gap and Urbach's energy Fig. 6 and Fig. 7    represents the optical band gap of direct allowed and indirect allowed transitions of Er3+  ions doped OCBT glasses. The physical and luminescence properties were investigated. The following are the conclusions drawn from the obtained results:• Various physical parameters have been evaluated by refractive index (nd ) and density (d).  • From absorption spectral analysis, J-O intensity parameters (Ωλ =2,4,6) and optical bandgap energies for both direct allowed and direct forbidden transitions have been calculated.  • By using J-O parameters various radiative properties such as radiative transition probability (AR ), total radiative transition probability (Aτ ), branching ratios (βR ), radiative lifetime (τR ) were evaluated.  • NIR emission spectral measurements, reveals a strong emission band around 1536 nm corresponding to the transition 4 I13/2 →4 I15/2 , from this stimulated emission cross-section , figure of merit (FWHM x σsec ), gain bandwidth (σsec  x Δλp), optical gain (σsec  x τR ) were measured.  • Decay spectral analysis were carried out under 522 nm for 1536 nm emission, all the prepared OCBTEr glasses flag up single exponential nature.  • From all the evaluated parameters, OCBTEr1.0 glass sample presents highest stimulated emission cross-section (σsec ) and high gain band width (σsec  x Δλp), large gain cross-section G(λ), which visualizes potential applications in solid state NIR lasers and EDFA applications.
