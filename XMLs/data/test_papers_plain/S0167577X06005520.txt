                  The Ca0.08Gd0.92VO4 crystal and Nd-doped Ca0.08Gd0.92VO4 crystals were grown by the Czochralski method; the lattice parameters of the crystals were determined by XRD. The results showed that substituting Nd3+ caused an increase in lattice parameter; the segregation coefficient of Nd3+ ions in the crystal is lower than that of Nd:GdVO4 crystal; the absorption spectra, fluorescence spectra, absorption cross-section and the emission cross-section of the Nd:Ca0.08Gd0.92VO4 crystal were investigated at room temperature; furthermore, the Raman performance of Ca0.08Gd0.92VO4 crystal was improved.

The Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal and Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals were grown by the Czochralski method; the lattice parameters of the crystals were determined by XRD. The results showed that substituting Nd3+  caused an increase in lattice parameter; the segregation coefficient of Nd3+  ions in the crystal is lower than that of Nd:GdVO4  crystal; the absorption spectra, fluorescence spectra, absorption cross-section and the emission cross-section of the Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal were investigated at room temperature; furthermore, the Raman performance of Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal was improved.   Keywords Czochralski method  Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal  Segregate coefficient  Crystal structure  Optical properties    1 Introduction Laser diode pumped solid-state lasers have a variety of applications such as in militaries, industries, medical treatments, and scientific research, due to their high level of stability, compactness, efficiency, and longevity. The Nd:GdVO4  crystal has excellent laser properties in high-power laser systems. It is better than that of Nd:YVO4 . Many laser experiments have shown that Nd:GdVO4  crystal is an efficient LD pumped solid-state laser crystal [1–6] . However, the intensity of Stimulated Raman Scattering (SRS) is quite weak for the GdVO4  crystal. In order to improve its Raman performance, the Ca-doped GdVO4  crystals have been grown and investigated in our laboratory. This paper deals with the effect of Calcium on Nd:GdVO4  single crystal.  2 Experimental 2.1 Materials synthesis The raw material, GdVO4 , was prepared by liquid-phase reaction. At first, NH4 VO3  (≥ 99% analytically pure) powder was dissolved in the heated distilled water, and then filtrated to clarify the solution. Secondly, Gd2 O3  (≥ 99.999% spectroscopic pure) powder was dissolved in the heated dilute nitric acid. Finally, the solutions of NH4 VO3  and Gd(NO3 )3  were mixed in a container equipped with a stirrer, and adjusted to pH = 7 by using ammonia. The chemical reaction equations are as follows:Gd2 O3  + 6HNO3  = 2Gd(NO3 )3  + 3H2 O   NH 4  VO 3  + 2 NH 3  · H 2  O   pH = 7   ( NH 4  )  3  VO 4  + H 2  O    Gd ( NO 3  )  3  + ( NH 4  )  3  VO 4    pH = 7   GdVO 4  ↓ + 3 NH 4  NO 3      The deposit of GdVO4  was washed by the distilled water, centrifugated, dried, pressed to sheet and sintered to obtained white polycrystalline materials. The synthesis method of NdVO4  is the same as GdVO4  (The starting materials, Nd2 O3 , is spectroscopic pure). Another material, Ca3 (VO4 )2 , was synthesized with NH4 VO3  and CaCO3 (≥ 99% analytically pure) by solid-phase reaction. The chemical reaction equations are as follows:2NH4 VO3  = V2 O5  + 2NH3 ↑ + H2 O↑; 3CaCO3  + V2 O5  = Ca3 (VO4 )2  + 3CO2 ↑    The well-proportioned admixture was pressed to sheet and sintered for 8 h at 850 °C, and then sintered at 1000 °C for 8 h. Finally we obtained white Ca3 (VO4 )2 .  2.2 Crystal growth All the crystals in this paper were grown by the Czochralski method with a radio frequency furnace heating an iridium crucible (50 mm in diameter and 45 mm in height) [7,8] .The growth atmosphere is N2  (purity: 99.99%, pressure: 0.14 MPa); in order to avoid the formation of the polycrystals in the process of crystal growth, it is necessary to remain at a temperature of 50–100 °C higher than the melting point of Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  polycrystalline materials for an hour. The pulling rate was 0.5 mm/h and the rotating velocity was 15–20 r/min. After crystallization, the crystals were pulled out of the melt and cooled down to room temperature at a speed of 8–100 °C/h. In the process of cooling, the atmosphere was supplied with 3–5 vol.% oxygen after the melt surface froze. Fig. 1   showed us the crystals and the wafer of the Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal.  2.3 Nd-doped concentration in the crystal The dopant concentration in the crystal was measured by JY-ULTIMA-2 (type)-ICP emission spectrum apparatus using the solution samples prepared previously. In the table, C l  was calculated according to the quantity of GdVO4 , Ca3 (VO4 )2 , NdVO4  in the melt, which is added to the iridium crucible at the beginning of crystal growth.  2.4 X-ray powder diffraction of the crystal The XRD patterns of Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals was taken with a computer-automated diffractometer (Rigaku D/max-3C).The lattice parameters of the crystals were calculated by X-pert plus program.  2.5 Polarized absorption spectrum measurement The polarized absorption spectrum of Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  was mensurated by Perkin–Elmer Lambda 35 UV/VIS spectrum apparatus in the wavelength range of 400 nm–1200 nm.  2.6 The Raman spectra of the crystal The Raman spectra of the crystal was measured by RERKZN-ELMER 2000 Raman spectra apparatus. Excitation wavelength is 1064 nm, power is 100 mW, and resolving power is 4.0 cm− 1 .   3 Results and discussion 3.1 Segregation coefficient The formula of the segregation coefficient is as follows [9] : k e   = C s  / Cl ,in which, C s  is the doped-ion concentration in the crystal and C l  is the doped-ion concentration in the melt. The segregation coefficients of Nd3+  and Ca2+  are shown in Table 1  . From Table 1 , the segregation coefficient of Nd3+  in Cax  Gd1 − x  VO4  crystals increases with the increasing of the Nd3+ -doped concentration, and the value of k Nd  in the Cax  Gd1 − x  VO4  crystal is less than 1.00, so C l  increases in growth process and C s  increases accordingly. Thus, the dopant concentration increases by degrees along the growth axis of the crystal. In order to reduce the concentration gradient of the doped ions in the crystal, it is an accepted method to descend the pulling rate and increase the rotative velocity step by step. The segregation coefficient (0.71) of Nd3+  in Cax  Gd1 − x  VO4  crystals is lower than that of GdVO4  crystals (0.78) [10] . According to k e   (Ca2+ ), we adjusted the value of C l  to keep the quantity of Ca2+  constant. Thus, the different Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals can be grown.  3.2 Lattice parameters The XRD pattern of Nd-doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals are shown in Fig. 2  .The lattice parameters of Nd: Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals are listed in Table 3 . So substituting Ca2+  benefits increasing the Nd-doped concentration in Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal, and the absorption of LD pumped lamp is enhanced.  3.5 Emission cross-section Based on the Judd–Ofelt theory, the emission cross-sections in this crystal were calculated. The emission cross-sections of Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals are listed in Table 7  . Through the Table 7 , we can know the value of σ em  · τ  of 1.48 at.% Nd3+ -doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal is biggest. The threshold value of laser is in inverse proportion to the value of σ em  · τ . That is to say, the bigger is the value of σ em  · τ , the more advantageous is the formation and output of laser. So 1.48 at.% Nd3+ -doped concentration is fittest in Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal. Although the emission cross-section of Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal are lower than it of Nd:GdVO4 [14,15] , there is no great effect of spectral parameter of the crystal. It is easy to realize laser output.  3.6 The Raman spectra of the crystal There is a sharp contrast of the Raman spectra between the GdVO4  crystal and the Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal through Figs. 8–11     . After choosing Ca as a substitute for gadolinium, the peak intensity of the Raman spectra of Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal was clearly stronger than the GdVO4  crystal at near 890 cm− 1 .This result indicates the Raman performance of Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal was improved.   4 Conclusions (1) The average value of the segregation coefficient of Nd3+  ions in Nd: Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystals is 0.71 which is lower than that of Nd:GdVO4  crystals. This indicates that substituting Ca2+  in Nd:GdVO4  crystals decreases the segregation coefficient of Nd3+  ions.  (2) XRD analysis showed that the Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal also belongs to a tetragonal system and has a Zircon-Type structure. Even after substituting Ca2+  and Nd3+ , its crystal structure has no enormous change.  (3) A 1.48 at.% Nd3+ -doped Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal has wider absorption peak at 809 nm. Its FWHM (10 nm) is bigger than FWHM (1.6 nm) [16]  of the Nd:GdVO4  crystal. It makes for GaAlAs laser diode pumped and debases the need of temperature of LD.  (4) Contrasting to Nd:GdVO4  crystal, the fluorescence lifetime of Nd:Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal was longer, and the emission cross-section and the value of τ  · σ  was smaller. So the crystal is easy to realize laser output.  (5) When Ca substituted for gadolinium, the Raman performance of Ca0.08 Gd0.92 VO4  crystal was improved.
